Understanding Floods

Essential facts about flooding to help you better prepare and respond

Flash Floods

Can develop in minutes with little visible rainfall, moving at speeds up to 30 mph with walls of water 10-20 feet high.

Property Damage

Just 1 inch of water can cause $25,000 in damage to a home. Most standard insurance doesn't cover flood damage.

Global Impact

Floods are the most common natural disaster worldwide, affecting over 2 billion people between 1998-2017.

Vehicle Danger

12 inches of water can float a car, and 24 inches will sweep most vehicles away, including SUVs and trucks.

Flood Preparedness Guide

Follow these steps to ensure you're ready before, during, and after a flood

1

Before a Flood

  • Know your flood risk and evacuation routes
  • Elevate critical utilities (electrical, HVAC)
  • Install check valves in plumbing
  • Waterproof your basement
  • Prepare sandbags or flood barriers
2

During a Flood

  • Move to higher ground immediately
  • Never walk or drive through floodwaters
  • Avoid contact with floodwater (may be contaminated)
  • Turn off electricity at the main breaker
  • Listen to emergency broadcasts
3

After a Flood

  • Wait for official clearance to return home
  • Document damage for insurance claims
  • Wear protective gear during cleanup
  • Disinfect everything floodwater touched
  • Watch for mold growth in coming weeks
