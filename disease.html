Understanding Viral Diseases

Essential facts about viral outbreaks to help you better prepare and respond

Transmission

Viruses can spread through droplets, aerosols, surfaces, and vectors. Some remain infectious on surfaces for days.

Reproduction Rate

The R0 (R-naught) measures contagiousness. Measles has R0 of 12-18, COVID-19 variants range from 5-8, flu is 1-2.

Vaccination

Vaccines prevent 4-5 million deaths yearly. Herd immunity typically requires 70-90% population immunity.

Pandemic Risk

Experts estimate a 47-57% chance of another COVID-level pandemic within 25 years due to zoonotic spillover risks.

Viral Disease Preparedness Guide

Follow these steps to ensure you're ready before, during, and after an outbreak

1

Before an Outbreak

  • Stay current with recommended vaccinations
  • Maintain 2-week supply of essentials
  • Identify reliable information sources
  • Practice proper hand hygiene techniques
  • Plan for remote work/school options
2

During an Outbreak

  • Follow public health guidance on masks/distancing
  • Disinfect high-touch surfaces daily
  • Monitor for symptoms and get tested
  • Isolate if symptomatic or exposed
  • Support vulnerable community members
3

After an Outbreak

  • Continue monitoring community spread
  • Get booster shots if recommended
  • Replenish emergency supplies
  • Review what worked in your preparedness
  • Advocate for better public health systems
