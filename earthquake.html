Understanding Earthquakes

Essential facts about seismic activity to help you better prepare

Tectonic Plates

Earth's crust consists of 15-20 major plates that move constantly, causing 80% of large earthquakes along their boundaries.

Richter Scale

Each whole number increase means 10x greater amplitude and ~32x more energy release. A 6.0 is 100x stronger than a 4.0.

Global Activity

About 500,000 detectable earthquakes occur annually, with 100 causing damage and one great quake (8.0+) each year on average.

Aftershocks

Can continue for weeks, months, or even years after the main shock, decreasing in frequency but potentially still dangerous.

Earthquake Preparedness Guide

Follow these steps to ensure you're ready before, during, and after an earthquake

1

Before an Earthquake

  • Secure heavy furniture to walls
  • Create an emergency kit (food, water, meds)
  • Identify safe spots in each room
  • Practice "Drop, Cover, Hold On" drills
  • Learn how to shut off utilities
2

During an Earthquake

  • Drop to hands and knees
  • Cover head and neck under sturdy furniture
  • Hold on until shaking stops
  • Stay indoors if inside, outdoors if outside
  • Avoid doorways and windows
3

After an Earthquake

  • Expect aftershocks
  • Check for injuries and hazards
  • Listen to emergency broadcasts
  • Use text messages instead of calls
  • Inspect utilities for damage

Effects of a 5.0 Magnitude Earthquake

Human Perception: Felt by nearly everyone; many awakened. Some dishes, windows broken.

Potential Damage: Damage negligible in buildings of good design and construction; slight to moderate in well-built ordinary structures.

Radius of Impact: Can be felt over areas as large as 1,000 km across.
