Understanding Landslides

Essential facts about landslides to help you better prepare and respond

Speed Variability

Landslides can move at speeds from mere millimeters per year to 200+ mph for debris flows - faster than a sprinting human.

Warning Signs

New cracks in ground/structures, tilted trees, sudden water flow changes, and faint rumbling sounds often precede landslides.

Rainfall Trigger

Just 3 days of steady rain can trigger landslides, with most occurring during intense rainfall or rapid snowmelt periods.

Global Impact

Landslides cause $3.5B in damage and 25-50 deaths annually in the U.S. alone, with higher impacts in mountainous regions.

Landslide Preparedness Guide

Follow these steps to ensure you're ready before, during, and after a landslide

1

Before a Landslide

  • Learn your area's landslide risk history
  • Consult a professional for retrofitting
  • Plant ground cover on slopes
  • Install flexible pipe fittings
  • Create an evacuation plan with routes
2

During a Landslide

  • Evacuate immediately if warned
  • Move to higher ground if possible
  • Avoid river valleys and low areas
  • Listen for unusual sounds
  • Stay away from slide path
3

After a Landslide

  • Stay away from slide area
  • Check for injured/trapped persons
  • Listen for emergency information
  • Watch for flooding
  • Report broken utilities
